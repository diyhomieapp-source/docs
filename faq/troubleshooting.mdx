Most DIYHomie issues are quick to resolve with a few straightforward steps. This page covers the most common app problems reported by users, with step-by-step instructions to get you back on track. If you work through the steps for your issue and still can't resolve it, our support team is ready to help — email us at [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com) or use the in-app feedback button at **Profile → Help & Feedback**.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I can't log in to my account">
    If you're having trouble signing in, work through these steps in order:

    1. **Double-check your email address.** Make sure you're using the email address associated with your DIYHomie account — if you signed up with Google or Apple, your login email may be different from what you typically use.
    2. **Reset your password.** On the login screen, tap **Forgot Password** and enter your email address. Check your inbox (and spam folder) for the reset link, which expires after 30 minutes.
    3. **Check your internet connection.** Make sure you're connected to Wi-Fi or a mobile data network before attempting to sign in. The login screen requires an active connection to authenticate.
    4. **Try a different sign-in method.** If you originally signed up with **Continue with Google** or **Continue with Apple**, use those buttons rather than entering a password manually — these accounts don't have a DIYHomie password.
    5. **Clear the app cache.** On Android: go to **Settings → Apps → DIYHomie → Storage → Clear Cache**. On iOS: delete and reinstall the app (your account data is cloud-stored and won't be lost).
    6. **Still stuck?** Contact support at [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com) with your account email address and a description of the error message you're seeing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My progress isn't syncing between devices">
    If your completed steps or saved projects aren't appearing on another device, here's how to troubleshoot:

    1. **Check your internet connection on both devices.** Progress syncs over the internet — both devices need to be connected for sync to occur. If you completed steps while offline, those will sync the next time that device has internet access.
    2. **Confirm you're signed in with the same account on both devices.** Go to **Profile** on each device and verify the email address shown is the same. If different accounts are signed in, your progress won't merge.
    3. **Force-close and reopen the app.** On both devices, fully close the DIYHomie app (swipe it away from the recent apps view), wait 10 seconds, and reopen. This triggers a fresh sync on launch.
    4. **Check the last sync time.** In the app, go to **Profile → Account Settings** — you'll see a "Last synced" timestamp. If sync is stuck, try toggling your internet connection off and back on, then relaunch the app.
    5. **Log out and log back in.** From **Profile → Account Settings → Sign Out**, sign out completely, then sign back in. This re-establishes your sync connection.
    6. **If the issue persists,** contact [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com) with both device types, OS versions, and your account email.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The AI assistant isn't responding">
    If the AI assistant stops responding, shows an error, or loads indefinitely, try these steps:

    1. **Check your internet connection.** The AI assistant requires an active internet connection to generate responses — it cannot function offline.
    2. **Close and reopen the chat.** Tap the back button to exit the current chat session, wait a moment, and start a new conversation. Occasionally a session can get stuck, and starting fresh resolves it.
    3. **Check your AI usage limit.** Free plan users have a monthly limit on AI assistant interactions. If you've reached your limit, you'll see a message indicating this. Your usage resets at the start of each billing month. To remove the limit, upgrade to DIYHomie Premium in **Profile → Subscription**.
    4. **Check for a service disruption.** If the AI is unresponsive for multiple users, it may be a temporary service issue on our end. Check [@DIYHomieApp](https://twitter.com/diyhomieapp) on X (Twitter) or our status page for updates.
    5. **Restart the app.** Force-close the app completely and reopen it, then try the AI assistant again.
    6. **Still not working?** Report the issue at [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com) and include a description of what you asked and what happened (or didn't happen) in response.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The app is crashing or freezing">
    App crashes are usually caused by an outdated app version, a full device cache, or a software conflict. Here's how to resolve them:

    1. **Update the app.** Open the App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android), search for DIYHomie, and install any available update. Most crash issues are resolved in app updates.
    2. **Restart your device.** A simple device restart clears memory and resolves many app stability issues. Power off your phone or tablet completely, wait 30 seconds, and power it back on.
    3. **Free up device storage.** If your device storage is nearly full (under 1 GB free), apps can crash frequently. Delete unused apps, photos, or files to free up space.
    4. **Clear the app cache (Android only).** Go to **Settings → Apps → DIYHomie → Storage → Clear Cache**. This removes temporary data that can occasionally cause instability without deleting your account data.
    5. **Reinstall the app.** If the above steps don't help, uninstall DIYHomie completely and reinstall it from the App Store or Google Play. Your account data, progress, and saved projects are stored in the cloud and will be fully restored when you sign back in.
    6. **Report persistent crashes.** If you experience repeated crashes on specific screens or actions, please report the issue at [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com). Include your device model, OS version, and a description of what you were doing when the crash occurred — this helps us identify and fix bugs faster.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I'm not receiving notifications">
    If you're not getting project reminders, new guide alerts, or other DIYHomie notifications, check these settings:

    1. **Check in-app notification settings.** Go to **Profile → Notifications** and confirm that the notification types you want are toggled on.
    2. **Check your OS notification permissions.** Your device's operating system must also have permission granted to DIYHomie to send notifications.
       - **iOS:** Go to **Settings → Notifications → DIYHomie** and make sure notifications are allowed, with your preferred alert style selected.
       - **Android:** Go to **Settings → Apps → DIYHomie → Notifications** and confirm notifications are enabled.
    3. **Check Do Not Disturb settings.** If your device is in Do Not Disturb mode, notifications are silenced at the OS level regardless of app settings.
    4. **Re-enable notifications.** If notifications are already enabled but still not appearing, try toggling them off and back on in both the app and OS settings, then restart the app.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A project guide seems incorrect or has an error">
    Our project guides are reviewed by experienced tradespeople and DIY professionals, but errors can occasionally slip through — and home improvement best practices evolve over time. If you find information that seems incorrect, outdated, or unclear, we want to know about it.

    To report an issue with a specific guide:
    1. Open the project guide in question.
    2. Tap the **⋯ (More)** menu in the top right corner.
    3. Select **Report an Issue** and choose the type of issue: incorrect information, unclear instructions, missing step, outdated content, or other.
    4. Add a brief description of what seems wrong and, if you know it, the correct information.
    5. Submit — our editorial team reviews all reports and updates guides typically within five to ten business days.

    You can also email feedback directly to [content@diyhomie.com](mailto:content@diyhomie.com) with the guide name and a description of the issue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I was charged but haven't received premium access">
    If your payment processed but your account still shows as Free tier, here's how to resolve it:

    1. **Restore purchases.** This is the most common fix. Go to **Profile → Subscription → Restore Purchases** and tap the button. This re-links your purchase to your DIYHomie account. Wait up to 60 seconds for your account status to update.
    2. **Check that you're signed in to the correct account.** Your Premium subscription is tied to the specific DIYHomie account that was active when you purchased. Make sure you're signed in with that account.
    3. **Check your purchase confirmation.** Look for a receipt in your email from Apple (for iOS purchases) or Google Play (for Android purchases). If the receipt shows the purchase was successful, the restore step above should resolve the account discrepancy.
    4. **Wait a few minutes.** In rare cases, there's a short delay between payment processing and account activation. Wait five minutes and then force-close and reopen the app.
    5. **Contact support.** If none of the above resolves the issue, email [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com) with your account email address, the date of purchase, and a screenshot of your purchase confirmation if available. We'll manually activate your account and ensure you're not double-charged.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't find a specific project in the library">
    If you're looking for a specific project and can't find it, try these approaches:

    1. **Use the AI assistant.** Ask the AI assistant directly — for example, "Show me how to replace a bathroom exhaust fan" or "I want to build a raised garden bed." The AI can pull up guides, summarize steps, and walk you through projects even if you don't know the exact guide title.
    2. **Try different search terms.** The search bar supports natural language — try searching by the problem ("leaky faucet"), the result ("tile backsplash"), or the material ("drywall repair"). If one term doesn't return results, try a synonym or broader category.
    3. **Browse by category.** From the home screen, tap **Browse** and filter by room (Kitchen, Bathroom, Outdoor, etc.) or project type (Repair, Upgrade, Maintenance, Install). Browsing categories often surfaces guides you wouldn't have found by search.
    4. **The project may be Premium-only.** Some guides are only available to Premium subscribers. If you see a guide with a lock icon, upgrading to Premium unlocks it along with the full library.
    5. **The project may not exist yet.** If you've tried all of the above and still can't find it, that guide may not be available yet. Submit a project request through **Profile → Suggest a Project** and our editorial team will review it — popular requests are typically published within four to six weeks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Contact Support

Our support team is available to help with any issue not covered here.

- **Email:** [support@diyhomie.com](mailto:support@diyhomie.com) — responses within one business day (Monday–Friday)
- **In-app:** Go to **Profile → Help & Feedback** to submit a request or report a bug directly from the app
- **Live chat:** Available to Premium subscribers through the web app at [app.diyhomie.com](https://app.diyhomie.com) during business hours (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–6 PM ET)

When contacting support about a technical issue, please include:
- Your device type and model (e.g., iPhone 15, Samsung Galaxy S23)
- Your operating system version (e.g., iOS 17.4, Android 14)
- Your app version number (found at **Profile → About**)
- A description of the issue and the steps that led to it
- Any error messages you're seeing (a screenshot is helpful)

<Note>
  The DIYHomie app and content library are updated regularly — typically every one to two weeks for app updates and weekly for new project guides and content improvements. Keeping your app updated to the latest version ensures the best performance, access to new features, and the most current project content. Enable automatic app updates in your device settings to stay current without thinking about it.
</Note>
