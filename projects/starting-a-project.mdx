Starting a project in DIYHomie means more than just reading instructions — it means walking into your work fully prepared. Every project guide gives you a complete picture of what's involved before you pick up a single tool, so you can gather what you need, set your expectations, and dive in with confidence.

## What's on a Project Detail Page

When you open a project from the library, you land on its **detail page**. This page gives you everything you need to evaluate and prepare before starting:

- **Overview** — A plain-language summary of the project, what it involves, and what the finished result looks like
- **Difficulty rating** — Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced, with a short explanation of why
- **Time estimate** — The typical time range to complete the project from start to finish
- **Tools list** — Every tool you'll need, with notes on optional substitutes where applicable
- **Materials list** — All supplies with approximate quantities, so you can shop accurately
- **Steps preview** — A numbered outline of the full process, so you know what's coming
- **Tips & warnings** — Highlighted callouts from experienced DIYers and tradespeople

Scroll the detail page at your own pace before committing to start — there's no timer running until you tap **Start Project**.

## How to Start a Project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the project">
    Find the project through Browse, Search, or your Saved Projects list and tap its card to open the detail page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the overview and difficulty rating">
    Read the project summary and check the difficulty level. If the rating is Intermediate or Advanced, scan the tips section for any prerequisites or cautions before proceeding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the tools and materials list">
    Go through the **Tools** and **Materials** tabs on the detail page. Make note of anything you don't already have on hand — you'll need these ready before you begin.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate your shopping list">
    Tap **Add to Shopping List** to send all required materials directly to your DIYHomie shopping list. You can review quantities, remove items you already own, and share the list before your trip to the hardware store. See [Shopping Lists](/tools-materials/shopping-lists) for details.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tap Start Project to begin step-by-step mode">
    When you're prepped and ready, tap the **Start Project** button at the bottom of the detail page. DIYHomie will launch the full-screen step-by-step view and log your start time automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step-by-Step Mode

Once you tap **Start Project**, DIYHomie switches into a focused, distraction-free view designed to guide you through the work in real time.

In step-by-step mode you'll find:

- **One step at a time** displayed in large, easy-to-read text — no scrolling through the whole guide
- **Photo references** for each step, showing exactly what your work should look like at that stage
- **Checkboxes** to mark each action within a step as done before moving forward
- **Inline tips** that surface context-sensitive advice as you progress
- **AI assistant access** via the chat bubble in the bottom corner — ask questions about the current step without leaving the guide

Tap **Next Step** when you're ready to advance. Your progress is saved automatically each time you move forward.

<Note>
  You can pause any project at any time and pick it back up later — even days or weeks later. DIYHomie saves your exact position, so you'll always resume right where you left off. See [Track Progress](/projects/tracking-progress) to learn how paused projects are managed.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Take a quick photo of your work at the end of each step. DIYHomie lets you attach photos directly to any step in your project log, giving you a personal record you can reference if something needs adjustment later — or just to admire how far you've come.
</Tip>
