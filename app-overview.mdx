DIYHomie is organized around a bottom navigation bar (mobile) or a left-side navigation panel (web) that gives you instant access to every part of the app from any screen. The layout is designed to get you to the right guide — or the right answer — as fast as possible, whether you're planning a project from the couch or troubleshooting something mid-task with paint on your hands. Here's what each section does and how to make the most of it.

## Main navigation sections

## Searching for a project

The **search bar** appears at the top of the Projects screen and is accessible from the Home Feed. You don't need to know the exact name of a repair or project — DIYHomie's search understands natural language, so you can search by:

- **Symptom** — e.g., "water dripping under sink", "outlet stopped working", "door won't close"
- **Room** — e.g., "bathroom", "garage", "basement"
- **Material or tool** — e.g., "caulk", "tile", "circuit breaker"
- **Task or project name** — e.g., "install ceiling fan", "paint trim", "fix drywall hole"

Results are ranked by relevance and filtered to match your saved experience level by default. Tap the **filter icon** next to the search bar to adjust difficulty, estimated time, or project category.

<Tip>
  The AI assistant is available on every screen via the floating **chat bubble button** in the bottom-right corner. You never have to navigate away from what you're doing to ask a question — tap the button, type your question, get your answer, and close the chat to pick up right where you left off.
</Tip>
