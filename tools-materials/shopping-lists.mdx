DIYHomie's Shopping List feature eliminates the frustration of mid-project hardware store trips. Every project in the app comes with a complete, auto-generated materials list that includes quantities, sizes, and estimated price ranges — so you can arrive at the store knowing exactly what you need and roughly what it'll cost. You can also build a list by chatting with the AI assistant, check off items you already own, and share the final list with a partner or export it to your phone before you head out.

## How Shopping Lists Are Generated

When you open a project, DIYHomie reads through every step and automatically identifies all required materials. Quantities are calculated based on standard project scope — for example, a bathroom tile project will calculate square footage and account for a standard overage buffer. You can adjust any quantity manually after the list is generated.

If you'd rather start from scratch or describe a custom job, ask the AI assistant to build a list for you. Just describe what you're trying to do and it will return a ready-to-use materials list.

## Generating a Shopping List

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a project">
    Navigate to the project you're working on from your **Projects** tab or the home screen. You can use any project from the library or one you've created yourself.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Generate Shopping List">
    On the project overview screen, tap the **Generate Shopping List** button. On web, you'll find it in the right-hand panel under **Project Tools**. The app processes the project steps and builds your list in seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review items">
    Browse through the generated list. Each item includes its quantity, recommended size or specification, and an estimated price range. Tap any item to edit the quantity or add a note (such as a preferred brand or color).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check off what you already have">
    Tap the checkbox next to any item you already own. Checked items are visually marked as "On Hand" and excluded from the running total, so you only see what you actually need to buy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export, share, or take to the store">
    When your list is ready, use the **Share** or **Export** button to send it, save it, or pull it up in-store. You can also leave the app open and use the list directly from your phone while you shop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What's Included for Each Item

Every item on your shopping list includes the following details:

- **Item name** — the standard name used at most hardware retailers
- **Quantity** — calculated from the project scope, with units (bags, linear feet, count, etc.)
- **Suggested size or spec** — for example, "2×4 × 8 ft," "1/2-inch drywall," or "20-amp GFCI outlet"
- **Estimated price range** — a typical retail range to help you budget before you shop

## Sharing Your List

You can share a shopping list with anyone — a partner picking up supplies, a contractor double-checking materials, or a friend who's helping with the project.

Sharing options include:

- **Text message** — sends a plain-text version of the list directly from your phone
- **Email** — generates a clean, formatted email with all items and quantities
- **Copy to clipboard** — pastes the full list as text so you can drop it anywhere

## Exporting Your List

Need a printed copy or a file you can save? Use the **Export** options:

- **PDF** — formatted for easy reading and printing, includes item names, quantities, specs, and price estimates
- **Plain text** — a simple, copy-paste-friendly version ideal for notes apps or messaging

<Note>
  Price estimates are based on average national retail data and are updated periodically. Actual prices may vary depending on your region, the retailer, current sales, and material grades. Always confirm pricing in-store or online before finalizing your budget.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Not working from a project template? Ask the AI assistant: **"What materials do I need to re-caulk my bathtub?"** It will generate a complete shopping list on the spot, which you can save directly to your lists for export or sharing.
</Tip>
