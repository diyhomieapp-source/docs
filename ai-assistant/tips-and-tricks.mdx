Most people use the DIYHomie AI Assistant to ask a quick question and get a quick answer — and that works great. But the assistant is capable of a lot more than simple Q&A. Once you know how to use it as a project-planning partner, a materials calculator, a permit advisor, and a real-time problem-solver, you'll find yourself reaching for it at every stage of a project, not just when something goes wrong. Here's how to get the most out of every conversation.

## Power User Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ask for Material Substitutions" icon="shuffle">
    If a guide calls for a material you don't have or can't find locally, ask the AI whether a substitute will work for your situation. For example: *"The guide says to use copper pipe for this supply line repair — can I use PEX instead?"* The AI will tell you whether the swap is compatible, what fittings or adapters you'll need, and whether there are any trade-offs to be aware of.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Request Simplified Explanations" icon="book-open">
    If the AI gives you an answer that feels too technical, just ask it to simplify. Say *"Can you explain that in simpler terms?"* or *"Explain that like I've never done any plumbing before."* The AI will restate the same information using plain language and everyday analogies, without making you feel like you missed something.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ask for Cost-Saving Alternatives" icon="piggy-bank">
    Before you commit to an approach, ask whether there's a more budget-friendly way to get the same result. Try prompts like *"Is there a cheaper way to do this that still holds up long-term?"* or *"What's the minimum I need to spend to fix this properly?"* The AI will flag where it's worth investing and where you can safely cut costs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Generate a Complete Shopping List Before You Start" icon="cart-shopping">
    Before you head to the hardware store, ask the AI to give you a complete list of everything you'll need. Prompt it with: *"What tools and materials do I need to replace a bathroom exhaust fan?"* You'll get a full list — including quantities, recommended specs, and common add-ons people forget — so you only make one trip.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ask 'Is This a Beginner Job?' to Gauge Difficulty" icon="gauge">
    Not sure if you're ready to tackle a specific repair? Ask the AI directly: *"Is replacing a light switch a beginner-level job?"* or *"How hard is it to install a ceiling fan if I've never done electrical work before?"* The AI will give you an honest difficulty rating, explain which parts of the job are straightforward and which require more care, and tell you if prior experience is genuinely needed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use the AI Mid-Project When You Hit a Snag" icon="circle-pause">
    The AI isn't just for planning — it's just as useful when you're already elbow-deep in a project and something unexpected happens. If a bolt is stripped, a pipe fitting doesn't match, or a step in the guide doesn't match what you're actually seeing, stop and describe the situation to the AI. It can adapt its guidance to your specific circumstances in real time, without you having to start the conversation over from scratch.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ask 'Do I Need a Permit?' Before You Start" icon="file-contract">
    Skipping a required permit can create serious problems when you sell your home or make an insurance claim. Before starting any significant project, ask the AI: *"Do I need a permit to finish my basement?"* or *"Is a permit required to replace an electrical panel?"* The AI will explain when permits are typically required and remind you to verify with your local building department, since requirements vary by municipality.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ask for Tool Alternatives When You're Missing Something" icon="toolbox">
    You don't always have to own every specialized tool a guide recommends. Ask the AI whether a common substitute will work: *"I don't have a pipe cutter — what else can I use to cut copper pipe?"* or *"Can I use a regular drill instead of a hammer drill for this anchor?"* The AI will tell you what alternatives are viable, what technique adjustments to make, and when a substitute simply won't do the job safely.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Conversation Continuity

The AI remembers everything discussed within your current session, so you never have to repeat yourself. If you described your bathroom layout three messages ago, the AI still has that context and will factor it into every answer it gives going forward. Take advantage of this by building on earlier messages with short follow-up prompts like *"What about the hot water line?"* or *"And how do I patch the ceiling once the leak is fixed?"* — no need to re-explain the full situation each time.

## Starting a Fresh Conversation

When you're done with one project and moving on to something completely different, it's a good idea to start a new conversation thread so the AI doesn't carry over context that no longer applies. To do this, tap the **New Chat** button (the pencil icon) at the top of the Chat screen. Your previous conversations are saved in your history and can be accessed any time by scrolling up in the Chat tab.

<Tip>
  When the AI gives you a response you want to save for later — a materials list, a step-by-step summary, or a cost estimate — tap the **bookmark icon** on that message to save it to your DIYHomie saved items. You can access your saved AI responses any time from your profile under **Saved → AI Responses**.
</Tip>
