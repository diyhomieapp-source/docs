The quality of the answer you get from the DIYHomie AI Assistant depends a lot on the quality of the question you ask. The AI is conversational and forgiving — you don't need to use technical jargon or follow a special format — but a little extra detail goes a long way toward getting a diagnosis or recommendation that's actually useful for your specific situation. The tips below will help you get the most accurate, actionable answers every time.

## Starting a Conversation

Tap the floating chat button, the **Chat** tab in the bottom navigation bar, or the **"Ask AI"** button on any project page to open the assistant. Once the chat window is open, just type your question as naturally as you would text a knowledgeable friend. There's no need to introduce yourself or explain how the app works — just dive straight into your question.

## Tips for Phrasing Great Questions

Keep these four principles in mind whenever you're asking the AI for help:

- **Be specific about the symptom or problem.** Instead of saying something is "broken" or "not working," describe exactly what it's doing — dripping, making a knocking noise, showing a specific error code, or emitting a smell.
- **Mention the room or location.** A leak under the kitchen sink is a different problem from a leak under a bathroom vanity. Location gives the AI important context about your plumbing layout, ventilation, and likely materials.
- **Include the material type if you know it.** Copper pipes, PVC pipes, and galvanized steel pipes all behave differently and require different repair approaches. Same goes for flooring, wall materials, and roofing types.
- **Say your skill level if you're unsure.** If you're a first-time DIYer, tell the AI — it will adjust the complexity of its instructions and flag steps that may be trickier than they look.

## Question Examples

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Plumbing: Dripping Faucet" icon="faucet-drip">
    **Too vague:** "My sink is broken."

    **Much better:** "My kitchen faucet is dripping constantly even when turned off — how do I fix it?"

    The second version tells the AI exactly where the faucet is, what it's doing, and when it's doing it. That's enough to narrow it down to a likely cause (worn cartridge or O-ring) and walk you through the right repair.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Painting: Primer Selection" icon="paint-roller">
    **Too vague:** "Paint help."

    **Much better:** "I'm painting my living room walls for the first time. What primer should I use on bare drywall?"

    Mentioning the surface type (bare drywall) and the room lets the AI recommend the right primer — in this case, a PVA drywall primer — rather than a generic answer that might not apply to your situation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Flooring: Cracked Tile" icon="border-none">
    **Too vague:** "Tile question."

    **Much better:** "I have 12×12 ceramic floor tiles in my bathroom and two of them are cracking. Can I replace just the broken ones, or do I need to redo the whole floor?"

    Tile size, material type, and room location all affect the answer. The AI can now tell you whether spot replacement is feasible, how to find matching tiles, and exactly how to remove the cracked ones without damaging the surrounding grout.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Electrical: Dead Outlet" icon="plug">
    **Too vague:** "My outlet stopped working."

    **Much better:** "One of the outlets in my home office stopped working overnight. Nothing tripped on my breaker panel and there's no GFCI outlet nearby that I can see. What should I check?"

    You've already told the AI what you've ruled out, which saves it from suggesting steps you've already taken and gets you to the real answer faster.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Following Up and Continuing a Conversation

You don't have to fit everything into one message. The AI remembers everything said earlier in your current session, so you can ask follow-up questions naturally:

- "Can you explain that last step in more detail?"
- "What if I don't have that tool?"
- "Is there a cheaper way to do this?"
- "What happens if I skip that step?"

Each reply builds on the context already established, so you never have to repeat your problem from scratch.

## Sharing Photos for Visual Diagnosis

If a picture would help explain the problem — a crack in the wall, a corroded pipe fitting, a confusing wiring setup — tap the **camera icon** in the chat input bar to attach a photo directly from your device's camera or photo library. The AI will analyze the image and factor it into its diagnosis. This is especially helpful for identifying damage patterns, material types, and the severity of structural or surface issues.

<Tip>
  You don't need to know any technical terminology to get great answers. Saying "the thing under my sink is leaking where the pipes connect" is perfectly fine — the AI understands plain language and will ask follow-up questions if it needs more detail.
</Tip>
