One of the most powerful things the DIYHomie AI Assistant can do is act as a first-responder when something in your home breaks or behaves unexpectedly. Instead of spending an hour searching forums and watching videos to figure out what's even wrong, you can describe what you're experiencing in plain language and let the AI walk you through a structured diagnosis. Once it identifies the most likely cause, it connects you directly to the relevant repair guide so you can get to work immediately.

## How the Symptom-to-Diagnosis Workflow Works

When you describe a problem to the AI, it follows a consistent process to make sure you get an accurate, relevant answer:

1. **You describe what you observe** — Tell the AI what you see, hear, smell, or feel. The more detail you include (location, material, when it started, how often it happens), the faster the AI can zero in on the cause.
2. **The AI asks clarifying questions** — Depending on how many possible causes match your description, the AI may ask one or two follow-up questions to rule out less likely explanations.
3. **You receive a diagnosis with a confidence level** — The AI presents the most likely cause of the problem along with a plain-language explanation of why it's happening, rated by confidence (e.g., "Most likely cause," "Possible cause," "Less likely but worth checking").
4. **The AI links to relevant project guides** — Once the diagnosis is confirmed, the AI surfaces the exact DIYHomie project guide that walks you through the repair, so you can move from diagnosis to action in one tap.

## Common Repair Categories

The AI assistant is trained to help you diagnose and address problems across all the most common home systems and surfaces.

### 🔧 Plumbing
- Dripping or leaking faucets and fixtures
- Slow drains and clogs
- Running or constantly cycling toilets
- Low water pressure throughout the house or in a single fixture
- Water heater issues (no hot water, inconsistent temperature, discolored water)

### ⚡ Electrical
- Outlets that have stopped working or feel warm to the touch
- Tripped breakers that won't reset or keep tripping repeatedly
- Flickering or dimming lights
- Dead switches that don't control any fixture

<Warning>
  Before inspecting or working on any electrical component, always switch off the circuit at your breaker panel. Use a non-contact voltage tester to confirm the power is off before touching any wires or connections. If you're unsure at any point, stop and call a licensed electrician.
</Warning>

### ❄️ HVAC
- Air conditioner running but not cooling the space
- Furnace failing to ignite or cycling on and off repeatedly
- Unusual noises from vents, the air handler, or the outdoor condenser unit
- Uneven heating or cooling between rooms

### 🏠 Structural
- Hairline cracks or larger fractures in drywall, plaster, or ceilings
- Squeaky or bouncy floors
- Doors and windows that stick, won't latch, or have developed gaps
- Water staining on walls or ceilings (indicating a leak source above)

### 🧺 Appliances
- Washer not draining, spinning, or filling
- Dryer running but not producing heat
- Dishwasher leaving dishes dirty, not draining, or leaking onto the floor
- Refrigerator not maintaining temperature or making unusual noises

## A Typical Repair Diagnosis Conversation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the AI Assistant">
    Tap the floating chat button or the **Chat** tab. If you're already on a project page related to your problem, tap **"Ask AI"** to open the assistant with project context already loaded.
  </Step>

    Type a clear description of the problem. Include the location, what you observe, and when it started. Example: *"There's a water stain spreading on my bedroom ceiling, directly below my upstairs bathroom."*

    The AI may ask questions like: *"Is the staining wet or dry right now?"* or *"Do you know if there's a bathtub or shower above the stain, or just a toilet?"* Answer as specifically as you can — these questions help narrow the diagnosis significantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the Diagnosis">
    The AI presents its most likely diagnosis, explains the likely cause, and rates its confidence. Read through the explanation so you understand what's happening before you start the repair.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Linked Repair Guide">
    Tap the project guide link the AI provides to jump directly into the step-by-step repair instructions. The guide opens with all the tools, materials, and steps you need to complete the fix.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example Conversation

```
You:   There's water staining on my ceiling below my upstairs bathroom.
       What's causing it?

AI:    Water staining on a ceiling below a bathroom typically points to
       one of three sources: a slow leak from the toilet's wax ring or
       supply line, a dripping drain connection under the bathtub or
       shower, or condensation from an uninsulated cold-water pipe.

       A couple of quick questions to narrow it down:

       1. Is the stain directly below the toilet, the tub/shower, or
          somewhere in between?
       2. Is the stain still wet or damp, or does it look old and dried?

You:   It's roughly below where the toilet sits, and it feels slightly
       damp when I press on the drywall.

AI:    A damp stain directly below a toilet almost always means the wax
       ring seal at the base of the toilet has failed. This is one of
       the most common — and most DIY-friendly — toilet repairs.

       The wax ring creates a watertight seal between the toilet and
       the drainpipe flange. When it fails, water escapes into the
       subfloor every time the toilet is flushed, which is why the
       stain stays damp.

       Confidence: High — this matches your symptoms closely.

       I've linked the "Replace a Toilet Wax Ring" guide below. You'll
       need about 90 minutes, a new wax ring (~$8), an adjustable
       wrench, and a bucket. Want me to walk you through the steps
       first, or would you like to jump straight to the guide?
```
