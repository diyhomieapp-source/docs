Your DIYHomie profile is more than just an account page — it's the foundation for everything the app personalizes on your behalf. Your skill level shapes the complexity of project instructions you see and the way the AI assistant explains things. Your home details help the app surface relevant guides and maintenance reminders. Keeping your profile accurate means better recommendations, more useful AI conversations, and a DIY experience that fits where you actually are in your homeownership journey.

## Profile Fields

You can view and edit the following information from your profile page:

- **Name** — your display name, used in the app and in any shared project lists
- **Profile photo** — upload a photo from your camera roll or take a new one in-app
- **Skill level** — choose from **Beginner**, **Intermediate**, or **Advanced** (see below for how this affects your experience)
- **Home type** — select the type of home you're working on: **House**, **Condo**, **Apartment**, or **Rental**

## Home Details

Filling in your home details helps DIYHomie tailor project guides and maintenance recommendations to your specific situation. You can add:

- **Year built** — used to surface era-specific guidance (for example, older homes may have knob-and-tube wiring or single-pane windows)
- **Square footage** — helps scale material estimates in cost and shopping list calculations
- **Number of bedrooms and bathrooms** — used to prioritize relevant project categories and seasonal maintenance checklists

You can leave any home detail blank, but providing them improves the accuracy of cost estimates and the relevance of project suggestions on your home screen.

## How Skill Level Affects Your Experience

Your selected skill level influences two key areas of the app:

**Project recommendations** — Beginner-level users see projects rated for low complexity first, with more detailed safety notes and tool explanations. Intermediate and Advanced users see a wider range of projects and shorter explanatory sidebars.

**AI assistant responses** — The AI tailors its language and depth to your skill level. At Beginner, it explains terminology and breaks down each step carefully. At Advanced, it assumes more baseline knowledge and gives more concise, technical answers.

You can change your skill level at any time — your history and saved projects are not affected.

## Updating Your Profile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    On mobile, tap the **profile icon** in the bottom navigation bar, then tap the **gear icon** in the top-right corner. On web, click your profile photo in the top-right corner and select **Account Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Edit Profile">
    Select **Edit Profile** to open the editable profile form. All current values are pre-filled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make your changes">
    Update any fields you'd like to change — name, photo, skill level, home type, or home details. To change your profile photo, tap the photo and choose **Upload from Library** or **Take Photo**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Tap **Save** (mobile) or click **Save Changes** (web). Your profile updates immediately across all devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Keep your skill level up to date as you gain experience. Accurate skill level settings lead to more relevant project recommendations and AI responses that match where you actually are — not where you started when you first created your account.
</Note>

## Deleting Your Account

<Accordion title="How to delete your DIYHomie account">
  Deleting your account is permanent. Before proceeding, note that the following will be removed and cannot be recovered:

  - Your profile and all account information
  - Saved projects and custom project notes
  - Shopping lists and cost estimates
  - AI conversation history
  - Any active Premium subscription (you will not be automatically refunded — cancel your subscription first through your app store or account settings)

  **To delete your account:**

  1. Go to **Settings** → **Account**
  2. Scroll to the bottom and tap **Delete Account**
  3. Confirm your password when prompted
  4. Tap **Confirm Delete** to permanently close your account

  If you're on a Premium plan, cancel your subscription before deleting your account to avoid being charged for the next billing cycle. See the [Subscription](/account/subscription) page for cancellation steps.
</Accordion>
